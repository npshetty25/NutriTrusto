                                                                                          ABSTRACT   `n                                         Research Abstract   `n                                                                                NutriTrusto is a smart pantry companion designed to reduce food waste and improve health awareness among urban college students. The solution combines real-time barcode nutrition transparency, pantry expiry tracking, and AI-based recipe suggestions to convert passive food ownership into active food management. This proposal follows Stanford d.School Phase 1 (Empathize & Define), using interview, observational, and survey data to frame a validated problem statement grounded in Health Belief Model, TAM, and Nudge Theory.   `n                                                                                Problem Space   `n                                         Household food waste + nutrition confusion   `n                                                                                Primary User   `n                                         Urban college students (18-25)   `n                                                                                Method   `n                                         Mixed-method design thinking approach   `n                                                                                Current Phase   `n                                         Empathize & Define (Proposal)   `n                                                                                                                       Barcode Health Scanner   `n                                         Instantly decodes packaged-food nutrition and highlights risk signals in plain language for fast student decisions.   `n                                         Smart Pantry Manager   `n                                         Tracks freshness timelines, triggers expiry nudges, and recommends recipes from available ingredients to reduce waste.   `n

                                                                                          LITERATURE REVIEW   `n                                         Underpinning Theories + Research Gap   `n                                                                                                            T1   `n                                         Health Belief Model   `n                                         Rosenstock, 1974   `n                                         Perceived risk and benefits influence preventive decisions in food and health behavior.   `n                                                                                                            T2   `n                                         Technology Acceptance Model   `n                                         Davis, 1989   `n                                         Perceived usefulness and ease of use shape adoption of digital food-assistance tools.   `n                                                                                                            T3   `n                                         Nudge Theory   `n                                         Thaler & Sunstein, 2008   `n                                         Low-friction prompts like expiry alerts can guide better decisions without coercion.   `n                                                                                                            RESEARCH GAP   `n                                         No prior student-centric Indian solution combines real-time nutritional transparency, pantry intelligence, and behavior nudges in one validated framework.   `n
